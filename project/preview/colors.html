four surfaces, one ink, seven jewels
I

The Four Grounds

Vellum is the marketing surface. Parchment, its quiet companion. Void and Ink are for product & technical canvases — the workshop at night.

grounds · α
Vellum
The default marketing surface. Warm ground, rests the eye.
#EDE3CF · ground · base
Parchment
The deeper leaf. Used beneath Vellum for section breaks and cards.
#D9CBAE · ground · alt
Void
The workshop at midnight. Product shell, code, and technical reference.
#0B0A0F · ground · deep
Ink
A shade warmer than Void. Sits beneath Void for layered panels.
#181624 · ground · layer
Rule of two
Any page uses at most two grounds: Vellum + Parchment, or Void + Ink. Never mix registers.
Contrast
Ink on Vellum: AAA. Bone on Void: AAA. Fade registers meet AA only.

III

The Brass Register

A six-step metal gradient used for casings, rivets, and primary buttons. Never as a text color. Never as a fill for plates.

brass · #B37E3E

mahogany · #2A1607
Use
Casings, navigation, primary buttons, rivets, compass rings, watch dials.
Never
Gradients on text. Gradients on plate fills. Brass as decorative fill with no metal structure.

Rule
A gemstone only appears when it means something. Emerald decorates nothing — it verifies.
Density
No more than three distinct gems per surface. More than three and the page becomes candy.
