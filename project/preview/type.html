EB Garamond — the body

Every long passage lives here. Wide measure, generous leading, pretty wrap, old-style figures.

prose · β

The recipe, not the kitchen

A manifesto, in three paragraphs.

AI models change — the ones we trust today will be replaced, likely within the year. Proven workflows do not. The useful thing to own is the recipe, stamped and sealed, portable across whatever oven the industry hands you next.

Babysitter was written to make those recipes first-class. Every agent call is wrapped in a convergent loop: plan, execute, verify, repeat. When the gates pass, a proof is issued — a receipt you can paste into a PR.

The alternative is a demo. A reel. A vibe. Our alternative is the seal.

Three things are never negotiable

— gates, determinism, the seal.

Gates are hard. A failed lint is not a suggestion. A red test is not "close enough." The system retries until convergence or it halts with a ruby verdict. No silent partial credit.

Determinism is cultural. Same agent, same plan, same gates — same outcome, or a written reason why not. Variance is a bug and we file it as one.

The seal, finally, is issued exactly once per task. It carries the commit, the gate log, the agent ID, the timestamp. Anything less is theater.
